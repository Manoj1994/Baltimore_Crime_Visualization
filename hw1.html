Crime in Baltimore

This is a data visualization about crime incidents in Baltimore from 2011 (December) to 2017 (September). I took the data from Kaggle and I used Tableau for visualization. I looked at some important features and generated reports specific to them. The features that have chosen are the time of day, type of crime, and spatial distribution of incidents.

Link to the Dataset : Batlimore Crime Dataset

The Baltimore Police Department (BPD) provides police services to the city of Baltimore, Maryland and is organized into ten districts. Here's the map:

Which district has the most criminal incidents?

The population of the city haven't changed much during the last five years, so its fine to aggregate crimes of different years. Here we can see north eastern and south eastern ditricts are top 2 districts that have hightest criminal incidents. Moreoever, the remaining districts have similar criminal incidents. The overall criminal record across all the districts doesnt vary much.

Most frequent criminal categories?

Incidents relating larceny, common assault and burglary occur more frequently than other categories. Where as incidents across homicide and other categories are far less compared to top categories. However, homicides per capita of baltimore is more than national average, which makes baltimore dangerous place to live.

How are criminal incidents across different neighbourhoods of the city?

Here, we can observe that more number of incidents occuring in neighbourhoods of central, eastern and north eastern districts. Apart from that, frequency of incidents in other neighbourhoods looks similar.

Any districts where particular crimes are increasing or decreasing?

For this analysis, I picked high rate criminal categories like Auto theft, larceny etc.. Here we can observe larceny incidents are more than other criminal incidents in all the districts. Burglary and common assualt are next two common categories across all districts. As we can see, other criminal categories remain in similar frequency across the districts.

Are there seasonal or time-of-week/day changes in crime occurrences?

Month/Year Plot

Here's the plot for total incidents in 6 years. we can observe similar trend in crimes over all the years. During the start of the year there are less crimes, they increase in middle and decrease by end of the year. This plot also shows increase in total crimes per day in recent years.

Daywise Plot

However, crimes remain neutral across all days of the month. There's no significant difference at starting or ending of the month.

Weekday Plot

Here's the chart for crimes committed per weekday. We can observe that crimes on weekdays are more frequent than weekends. May be people roam a lot on weekdays, which gives a plenty of room for vehicle theft and larceny.

Hourly Plot

That's obvious, the crime frequency in the night looks less compared to day. Incident frequency picks up as day passes by.

Conclusion:

Overall, the crime in baltimore seems little high than national average. The weekwise, daywise and monthwise crime remains almost similar. There are no seasonal crimes in baltimore. Larceny and burglary incidents seems to happen frequently across all the neighbourhoods. East and central side of baltimore seems to be pretty dangerous to live. If we observe the map of baltimore, crime happens throught the city and mostly the crime rate across all the states remain same. The most used weapon are hands, as most of the crimes are related to larceny and burglary.
